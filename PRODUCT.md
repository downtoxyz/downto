# down to — Product Doc

## What it is

A lightweight coordination layer for making plans with friends. Not a group chat, not an event app, not a social feed — it's the moment before plans happen.

## Who it's for

Both sides of the equation:
- **The initiator** — has an idea or found an event, wants to see who's down without the overhead of texting five group chats
- **The joiner** — wants to go out but doesn't want to organize anything, just wants to raise their hand

## Core problem

**Social friction kills plans.** People want to hang out but nobody wants to be the one to initiate. By the time someone texts the group chat, coordinates schedules, and picks a plan — the moment has passed. Meanwhile, events are scattered across IG stories and word of mouth, and you never know which friends would've been down.

## The magic moment

**Seeing who's down.** You post a check or save an event and watch your friends light up as interested. The app removes the awkwardness of "would anyone even want to do this?" — you find out without asking directly.

## Core loops

### Loop 1: Check → Squad → Plans
1. I have an idea ("who wants to do a movie night?") or find an event (paste an IG link)
2. My friends see it and tap "down" or "maybe"
3. A squad forms — small group, focused chat
4. We lock in a date/time and go

### Loop 2: Browse → Discover → Join
1. I open the app and see what friends (and friends-of-friends) are interested in
2. I see something I'm down for and tap in
3. I get pulled into a squad with people I know (or almost know)
4. I might meet someone new through a mutual friend

### Loop 3: Invite → Connect → Expand
1. A friend sends me an invite link
2. I sign up already connected to someone
3. I see their checks and their friends' checks
4. My social circle on the app grows organically through real connections

## What success looks like

A good week for a user means any of these happened:
- A check turned into real plans with a squad
- They stayed in the loop on what friends are doing, even if they didn't go out
- They connected with a friend-of-friend through a shared interest

## What the app is NOT

- **Not another group chat.** Squad chat is for logistics ("where should we meet?"), not hanging out. The plan should move to real life.
- **Not an event listing app.** Events are a starting point for coordination, not the product. The product is "are you down?"
- **Not a social media feed.** No posting for clout, no likes, no followers. It's about making plans.
- **Not an advertising platform.** Events come from users and their networks, not promoters or brands.

## Interest checks are the core

Events started as the primary object, but interest checks are becoming the real product. An event is just one type of thing you can check interest for. The core mechanic is: **throw something out, see who bites, form a group, make it happen.**

## Current stage

Friends & family testing. Core features work, iterating based on real usage. Growth is through invite links — you join because a friend brought you in.

## Open product questions

- How far should the friend-of-friend network extend? (2 hops? Capped?)
- Should checks have categories/vibes, or stay freeform?
- What's the right squad size before it stops feeling intimate?
- How aggressive should expiry be? (Checks, squads, chats)
